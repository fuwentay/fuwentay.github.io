My journey

Since I was in high school, I have always loved math and physics — regularly participating in the Olympiads, and any math or science-related workshops.

Although I already had an interest, participating in the Singapore Young Physicist Tournament was what spurred it further. It was a scientific debate competition where students had to hypothesise the explanation to a phenomenon, come up with a theoretical model, and validate it, while fighting off criticism from other teams. Participating in 2 iterations of the competition, I investigated problems such as the paradoxical sinking motion of bubbles in a column of oscillating fluid, and the motion of a real Newton's cradle with Hertzian losses to name a few.

Through this, I developed a deep interest in research and aspired to be an applied researcher at the Agency for Science, Technology and Research (A*STAR) — where I ended up interning at for almost 2 years, building a smart mattress to alleviate pressure ulcer formation (that has been patented under the agency).

However, as I did more hardware and firmware internships, I realised that I preferred to work on software-related projects. Because I found hardware to have too many degrees of freedom and in turn more room for error, whereas I enjoyed building the logic — the "brain" of the system.

Through the Covid-19 pandemic, information regarding investing and trading become more accessible and public as there was an insurgence of trading gurus and financial advisors online. That was when I created my first expert advisor on MetaTrader4 using moving average crossovers and the relative strength indicator — that unfortunately had no alpha.

Since then, I've been exploring the world of quantitative finance, trying to seek alpha.
